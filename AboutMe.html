I've always had a soft spot for coding, I started out my adult life at Plymouth university studying Game Art & Design, I mostly did the art side of game dev as it was the most appealing to me back then. I'd always manage to do some coding in my projects though, whether it was a small bit of C++ or in the visual blueprints that Unreal Engine has to offer.

I had discovered at the end of my degree that the spark had disappeared for game art, but my urge to create was still present. A year on from graduating I discovered a course for web development and a new spark lit up in me. Fast-forward to now, I am loving coding and eager to level up my portfolio and skillsets more than ever!

My focus currently is building my skills up so I can comfortably escape calling myself a "junior dev". I'm always learning obscure things and reading useful articles.
